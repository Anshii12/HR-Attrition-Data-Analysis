## Project Overview
As an HR professional transitioning into Data Analytics, I developed this interactive dashboard to answer critical business questions regarding workforce demographics, pay-for-performance alignment, and regional talent distribution. This project demonstrates my ability to transform raw HR data into actionable insights using Advanced Excel and data visualization techniques.

## Tech Stack
1. Analysis Tool: Microsoft Excel (Pivot Tables)
2. Visualization: Interactive Excel Dashboards (Slicers, Dynamic Charts)
3. Key Skills: Demographic Segmentation, Compensation Benchmarking

# 1. How is our talent distributed across age groups?
Insight: The majority of the workforce falls within the 30–49 age range. There is a sharp drop-off in employees under 20 and over 60, suggesting a need for early-career recruitment and succession planning.

# 2. Is our compensation strategy aligned with performance?
Insight: Yes. Across all departments, "Top" performers consistently earn the highest average salaries, confirming that the organization successfully operates a pay-for-performance model.

# 3. Which departments are the most "talent-expensive"?
Insight: Human Resources and Finance lead in average salary for top performers (near $140,000), while Operations and Customer Support have lower salary ceilings.

# 4. What does our gender and tenure diversity look like?
Insight: In most regions, the largest group of employees has less than 10 years of tenure. While regions like the Southeast show a perfect gender balance (39F/39M), other regions like the West show a significant male-to-female gap among newer hires.

## Regional Analysis Highlights
The dashboard allows for deep-dives into specific geographical segments:

- Midwest: High focus on Leadership roles with a balanced gender ratio.
- Northeast: Finance-heavy region with a seasoned workforce (40-49 age group).
- Southwest: Notable for a slight female-leaning workforce and specific pay anomalies in Marketing.
- West: Represents the highest growth but also the largest gender disparity in the 0-9 year tenure bracket.


## How to Use
1. Download the .xlsx file.
2. Open in Excel and ensure "Enable Content" is selected for the slicers.
3. Use the Region, Department, and Manager filters to explore the data dynamically.
